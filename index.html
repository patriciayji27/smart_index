Reading the Vol Surface
Beyond a Single Number

Equity index vol surfaces encode rich market expectations — about tail risk, term structure of uncertainty, macro event pricing, and dealer positioning — that go far beyond VIX. This project builds a toolkit to systematically extract, track, and analyse those signals.

Surface Features
What the Surface Encodes
The raw surface is high-dimensional. We compress it into a small set of economically interpretable features — each capturing a distinct aspect of how the market prices risk.
Feature 01

Skew (25Δ Risk Reversal)

skew = IV(25Δ put) − IV(25Δ call)

Measures the relative cost of downside vs. upside protection. Steep skew → expensive crash insurance. For SPX, skew is persistently negative (puts > calls) reflecting structural demand for tail hedging. The level is well-known; the interesting signal is in skew changes conditional on regime.

−8.2 vol pts
Typical 30d SPX skew
Feature 02

Term Structure Slope

slope = IV(90d, ATM) − IV(30d, ATM)

Positive slope (contango) is the normal state — longer-dated vol is higher because more can happen. Inversion (near > far) signals acute near-term stress. The speed of transition from contango to backwardation may matter more than the level.

+1.8 vol pts
Normal contango (positive)
Feature 03

Smile Convexity (Butterfly)

cvx = (IV_25Δp + IV_25Δc) / 2 − IV_ATM

Approximates the second derivative of the smile — how expensive wings are relative to ATM. High convexity → the market is pricing fat tails. Low convexity → complacent on extremes. This captures positioning/hedging demand that skew alone misses.

+2.4 vol pts
Wing premium above ATM
Feature 04

Vol Risk Premium

VRP = IV(30d) − RV(30d realised)

The wedge between what the market expects and what materialises. On average positive (vol sellers get compensated), but varies hugely by regime and tenor. The interesting question: is the VRP compensation for risk or a structural flow artifact?

+3.5 vol pts
Average implied – realised spread

Methodology
Regime Rules
Current implementation uses heuristic thresholds as a first pass. HMM and clustering overlays are planned.

Heuristic Approach

The baseline classifier uses VIX level as the primary axis, with term structure ratio and skew as confirmation signals. This is deliberately simple — the goal is a transparent, interpretable first pass before layering on statistical models.

# From config/regime_rules.yaml if vix < 15: regime = "low_vol" elif vix < 22: regime = "normal" elif vix < 30: regime = "elevated" else: regime = "crisis" # Term structure confirmation if vix_ratio > 1.05: flag = "backwardation" elif vix_ratio < 0.95: flag = "contango"

Why Start Simple

Complex models (HMMs, clustering) can overfit to noise and are hard to interpret in real time. Starting with heuristic rules forces us to think about what we actually believe about market states before letting a model tell us. The planned approach:

① Heuristic rules — transparent, interpretable
② HMM on surface features — data-driven state discovery
③ Compare & reconcile — where do they disagree?
④ Conditional analysis — feature behaviour by regime
Event Studies
How the Surface Responds to Macro Events
Scheduled macro releases (FOMC, CPI, NFP) create predictable spikes and collapses in implied vol. The question isn't whether vol rises pre-event — it's whether the pricing is systematically biased, and whether that bias depends on regime.

Framework
Event Study Design

What We Measure

For each event instance:

# Implied move vs. realised move implied_move = straddle_price / spot # break-even realised_move = abs(close[t+1] / close[t] - 1) # Surface deformation skew_pre = skew_25d(surface[t-1]) skew_post = skew_25d(surface[t+1]) Δskew = skew_post - skew_pre # Conditioning on regime at time of event regime = classify(vix[t], ts_ratio[t], skew[t])

Key Questions

Is the implied move too large?
Compare the pre-event straddle break-even to the actual move across all instances. Distribution of errors — symmetric or one-sided?

Does regime matter?
Does the market overprice FOMC in low-vol (complacent) regimes and underprice in high-vol (anxious) regimes?

Dot-plot vs. non-dot-plot
FOMC meetings with new projections (Mar/Jun/Sep/Dec) should carry more information. Does vol pricing reflect this asymmetry?
